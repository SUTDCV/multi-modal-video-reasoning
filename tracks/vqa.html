ICCV 2021 Workshop

Track 1: Video Question Answering

- Video Question Answering -

Video question answering, which aims at answering question about the video content, has emerged as an important research topic among vision-and-language tasks, since to accurately answer the question, models need to perform spatio-temporal, logical reasoning over the video content. Such video reasoning capability lies in the core of Artifical Intelligence study.
However, the video reasoning capability of intelligent models is less explored in existing benchmarks for video question answering. Hence, to facilitate the research of causal, logical reasoning in videos, we introduce a new comprehensive VideoQA benchmark, SUTD-TrafficQA, which focuses on traffic-event based video reasoning. We include 6 challenging video reasoning tasks (e.g, counterfactual inference, event forecasting, introspection) with 10,080 real-world videos covering diverse traffic scenarios and 62,535 QA pairs. Samples and details please refer to our CVPR2021 paper and SUTD-TrafficQA.
In this challenge, we invite interested researchers to evaluate their novel VideoQA models on our SUTD-TrafficQA dataset. The top ranking teams will be invited to present their works in our workshop.

- Registration -

All teams are required to register before the registration deadline by filling and submitting the Registration Form.

- Data Download -

When the Challenge opens and if you have registered for the participation, we will send the dataset download link to your registration email. You can use the received link to download our dataset directly without requesting for download in our dataset homepage. What's more, we provide detailed explanation and examples in our dataset homepage SUTD-TrafficQA to show how to use our dataset, including annotation files and pre-extracted video features.

- Submission -

Each team is allowed to submit three times. The submission file should be a .csv file, containing two columns. The first column is the record_id of the testing data , and the second column is the predicted answer index of corresponding testing sample. The submission filename should contain: 1) Track number; 2) Team name; 3) Abbreviated model name; 4) Number of submissions . See submission example Track1-SUTDCV-Eclipse-2.csv (NOTE: Headers "record_id" and "predicted_answer" are required).
Please send your .csv file to mmvrac.trafficqa@gmail.com within the submission period. Any submissions beyond the submission period will be ignored.

- Evaluation -

The evaluation metric is the answering accuracy, meaning that your submitted answer predictions are compared to the ground-truth answers. The accuracy is computed by num_correct_predictions / num_testing_samples.
